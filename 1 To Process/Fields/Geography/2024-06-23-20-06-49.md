Sure, here are some common components found in place names around the world (excluding Europe), often reflecting geographical features, historical events, or cultural influences:

### North America

#### United States and Canada

- **-ton** (town): Charleston, Houston
- **-ville** (town): Louisville, Knoxville
- **-burg** or **-borough** (town or fortified place): Pittsburgh, Greensboro
- **-field** (field or open land): Springfield, Bakersfield
- **-ford** (river crossing): Hartford, Medford
- **-wood** (forest or wood): Hollywood, Inglewood
- **-lake** (near a lake): Salt Lake City, Clear Lake
- **-creek** (near a stream or creek): Walnut Creek, Battle Creek

### Latin America

#### Mexico, Central, and South America

- **-tlan** (place of in Nahuatl): Mazatlán, Cihuatlán
- **-co** (place of in Nahuatl): Mexico, Acapulco
- **-la** or **-las** (the in Spanish): Guadalajara, Puebla de Zaragoza
- **-del** (of the in Spanish): Ciudad del Carmen, Valle del Bravo
- **-rio** (river): Rio de Janeiro, Puerto Rico

### Asia

#### China

- **-cheng** (city): Guangzhou, Wucheng
- **-zhou** (administrative division): Hangzhou, Suzhou
- **-jing** (capital): Beijing, Nanjing
- **-shan** (mountain): Huangshan, Taishan

#### Japan

- **-kyo** (capital): Tokyo, Kyoto
- **-shima** (island): Hiroshima, Fukushima
- **-mura** (village): Kamimura, Shimomura
- **-yama** or **-san** (mountain): Fujiyama, Asamayama

#### India

- **-pur** or **-pura** (city or town): Jaipur, Nagpur
- **-abad** (city): Hyderabad, Ahmadabad
- **-garh** (fort): Chandigarh, Gwaliorgarh
- **-nagar** (city): Bengaluru Nagar, Surat Nagar

### Africa

#### Arabic-speaking Countries

- **-el** or **-al** (the in Arabic): Algiers, Alexandria
- **-medina** (city): Medina, Fes el Bali
- **-dar** (house): Dar es Salaam, Dar es Salam
- **-jebel** (mountain): Jebel Ali, Jebel Barkal

#### Sub-Saharan Africa

- **-burg** (town or fortified place): Johannesburg, Vryburg
- **-ville** (town or city in French-speaking regions): Brazzaville, Libreville
- **-kraal** (village or enclosure in Afrikaans): Pietermaritzburg, Klerksdorp

### Australia and New Zealand

- **-borough** or **-burg** (town): Yarraburg, Yarrawonga
- **-ton** (town): Melbourne, Hamilton
- **-vale** (valley): Morwell, Yass Valley
- **-bay** (coastal area): Byron Bay, Hervey Bay

These naming conventions help identify the historical, geographical, or cultural contexts of different places around the world, providing a fascinating glimpse into the local heritage and influences.
